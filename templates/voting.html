Long Range Planning Requirements
18+ months out — Strategic capacity, supplier portfolio, network planning
avg 3.0
1
Source of Truth for Long Range Plan
Establish a single, system-based source of truth for the long-range plan — replacing PowerPoints and disconnected spreadsheets
Critical

Today the 10-year strategic plan lives in a PowerPoint and long-range supply numbers are assembled ad-hoc. Nike needs a single, system-based source of truth for the long-range plan — with versioning, traceability, and the ability to link strategic bets to downstream capacity and supplier commitments.

Why it matters

Without a system of record, there is no traceability from strategic intent to capacity reservations to seasonal plans. A PowerPoint cannot be queried, versioned, or connected to execution. Moving the long-range plan into the system enables the organization to evaluate whether strategic bets are translating into operational reality.

2
Connected to Nike’s Operating Plan
Link the long-range supply plan to Nike’s corporate operating plan for aligned capacity and investment decisions
Critical

The long-range supply plan must be connected to Nike’s corporate operating plan so that capacity reservations, supplier portfolio decisions, and network investments are aligned with the company’s financial and strategic commitments — not planned in isolation.

Why it matters

When the long-range supply plan is disconnected from the operating plan, capacity investments and supplier commitments can drift from corporate priorities. Connecting them ensures that long-range supply decisions reflect the company’s strategic direction and financial guardrails.

3
Separation of Demand and Supply Plan
Maintain an independent supply plan distinct from the demand forecast at long range
Critical

Define the level of independence between long-range demand forecasts and supply capacity commitments. Today these are conflated; the supply plan should account for capacity reservations, strategic supplier commitments, and network positioning independently of the demand forecast.

Why it matters

Without a clear separation, long-range supply decisions are driven by demand projections that will inevitably change. The supply plan must reflect what Nike can commit to — factory capacity, supplier portfolios, and network footprint — independent of demand volatility.

4
Long Range Decision Framework
Define what decisions are made at the long-range horizon and build explicit system support for them
High

Today Nike scrambles to produce long-range supply numbers. Define what decisions are actually being made at the long-range horizon — capacity reservations, supplier portfolio strategy, network footprint planning — and build explicit system support for those decisions.

Why it matters

If we don’t define the decisions, we can’t support them. Long-range planning should answer: what capacity do we need to reserve? Which suppliers are strategic? What does our network look like in 3–5 years? Today these answers live in ad-hoc spreadsheets and presentations.

Mid Range Planning Requirements
18 to 9 months (Wk70 to ~Wk36) — Product creation, style-level decisions, factory development
avg 3.0
1
Product Creation Integration (Wk70 Onward)
Connect first demand signals at 18 months to capacity and sourcing decisions
Critical

When Nike's product creation process starts at 18 months before season, the planning system must shift into a different gear. First demand signals at Wk70 need to flow into capacity and sourcing decisions immediately — this is when the engine transitions from strategic to tactical.

Why it matters

Wk70 is the first demand signal. Between 18 months and 9 months, decisions compound rapidly. If the planning system doesn't engage at product creation kickoff, downstream decisions are made without supply awareness, leading to capacity surprises and missed commitments.

2
Style-Level Decision Support
System support for significant style-level decisions: factory assignment, development allocation
Critical

Significant decisions are made at the style level during mid-range: which factory develops and produces a new model, development system allocation, and factory development assignments. Some of these decisions are irreversible — development of a style locks in factory partnerships and tooling investments.

Why it matters

Today these decisions are made with limited system support. The planning system must provide data-driven recommendations for factory-style matching, considering factory capabilities, capacity availability, cost, and strategic alignment.

3
Nike Flow / Factory Development Portal Integration
Connect PMD/Nike Flow with planning ecosystem for mid-range visibility
High

PMD is transitioning into Nike Flow. New model assignment, factory development, and the development system need to be connected to the planning ecosystem so mid-range decisions are visible and traceable. Nike Flow would be the portal where factories engage with development assignments.

Why it matters

Without integration, factory development decisions happen in isolation from capacity and supply planning. Connecting Nike Flow to the planning ecosystem ensures that when a style is assigned to a factory, capacity implications are immediately visible.

4
Regionalization & Duty-Differentiated Planning
Account for regional sourcing strategies, duty optimization, and geo-specific allocation
High

Nike is shifting from a global marketplace model to more regionalization with duty differentiation. Mid-range planning must account for regional sourcing strategies, duty optimization, and geo-specific factory allocation — decisions that need to be made well before the short-range execution window.

Why it matters

Previously Nike operated as a global marketplace. Increasing regionalization and duty considerations mean that where product is sourced and routed must be planned at mid-range, not decided reactively during execution.

5
Franchise & Model Scenario Planning
Evaluate franchise-level and model-level strategic bets before committing capacity
High

Enable franchise-level and model-level scenario planning during mid-range to evaluate strategic bets before committing to factory capacity and development resources. What happens if we double down on a franchise? What if a new model underperforms?

Why it matters

Mid-range is where Nike makes bets on franchises and models. Without scenario planning at this horizon, commitments are made based on single-point forecasts with no visibility to the range of outcomes.

★ Cross-Vendor Principles (Recurring Themes)

  • Progressive commitment as the default — don’t lock everything in at once
  • Range-based signals, not point forecasts — communicate uncertainty honestly
  • Constrained plan governs demand — what can actually be made trumps what is wished for
  • Standard backbone, heterogeneous execution — one system, different factory workflows
  • Plan at execution level, aggregate upward — not the other way around
  • Trust is an investment, not a byproduct — accuracy and transparency build partner confidence
