Critical
▼

Configurable Planning Logic Beyond Simplistic JIT
Critical
▼

Replace the current overly simplistic Just-In-Time engine logic with configurable planning modes: consumption-based, buy-date aligned, hybrid, and segmented approaches driven by product characteristics and business model requirements.

Why it matters

The current JIT logic uses weekly consumption and works back from FG GAC date, making it difficult to reconcile with FG plans and Nike buy-date logic. This mismatch cascades downstream to materials planning and factory coordination.

Capacity-Aware Constrained Production Planning
Critical
▼

The solver must model global factory capacity at the right granularity (basic, medium, high complexity) and constrain production plans accordingly — including network modeling, netting, minimums handling, and demand/supply match across the entire supply network.

Why it matters

Early, fixed purchase commitments without capacity awareness drive over-buying, prebuilds without precision, and excess weeks of supply. The current engine forces decisions months before real demand is known, tying up ~$650M per year in excess inventory.

5
Independent Supply Plan (Decoupled from Demand Plan)
High
▼

Create a true supply plan that is distinct from the demand forecast. The supply plan accounts for safety stock, promotional builds, capacity pre-commitments, and supplier constraints — producing different numbers than the forecast for the same SKU and horizon.

Why it matters

Organizations conflate forecast and plan, leading to misaligned expectations. For SKU 58125589, the forecast might show 781,794 units while the plan shows 1,013,832 — because the plan includes safety stock, prebuilds, and capacity reservations. Today the supply plan is tied directly to the demand plan with no separation.

Stakeholder references
Kevin — GSP First Principles Research

Supply Shaping Actions (Prebuild, Re-Phase, Defer)
High
▼

Built-in capabilities for proactively recommending supply shaping actions — prebuild, re-phasing, or deferral — early in the planning horizon to reduce buy risk, rather than reacting after commitments are locked.

Why it matters

Earlier decision-making shifts critical actions upstream where outcomes can still be influenced, reducing last-minute changes, execution fire drills, and late decision making (chase). The plan exists to bridge the tension between commitment and flexibility.

AI-Powered Unplanned Resolution Automation
Automate triage and resolution of unplanned demand exceptions using AI agents
Start Now
▼

What & Why

Excessive Unplanned and Build Late effort drives rework, missed delivery windows, and contributes to ~$488M/year in gross margin loss. Today, planner effort is spent firefighting instead of shaping the plan. An AI agent can classify unplanned exceptions by root cause, recommend the optimal resolution (reroute, prebuild, defer, cancel), and auto-execute low-risk resolutions with human approval for high-impact ones.

Impact: Reduce UP% by 15-25%, save 200+ planner hours/season

Implementation Approach

  • Train classifier on 2+ years of historical unplanned resolutions and outcomes
  • Build rule-based triage for common patterns (late BOM, capacity overflow, booking bubble)
  • Layer ML recommendations for complex multi-factor exceptions
  • Human-in-the-loop approval for resolutions above financial threshold
  • Integrate with existing APO exception workflow as an overlay, not a replacement

Prerequisites

  • Access to historical exception data and resolution outcomes
  • Defined exception taxonomy and resolution action catalog
  • API access to current APO exception queue

Stakeholder Alignment

Directly addresses Kevin's "Exception management visibility" and Li's "Get rid of booking bubbles" priorities. Aligns with the ecosystem architecture's Intelligence + Automation layer.

What & Why

People use "forecast" and "plan" interchangeably, leading to misaligned expectations. The first principles research showed that for the same SKU, the forecast might show 781K units while the plan shows 1.01M — a 30% gap that's expected but poorly understood. A reconciliation tool showing the gap breakdown (safety stock, prebuilds, capacity reservations, minimums) builds organizational literacy and reduces finger-pointing.

Impact: Align cross-functional understanding, reduce escalations

Implementation Approach

  • Build waterfall visualization: Forecast → Safety Stock → Prebuilds → Capacity Adj → Minimums → Plan
  • Automate variance driver decomposition at style/SKU level
  • Provide seasonal trend view of forecast-to-plan gap evolution
  • Include in standard planning review cadence

Prerequisites

  • Access to both demand forecast and supply plan data
  • Definition of variance driver categories

Stakeholder Alignment

Addresses Alvin's #1 challenge ("Lack of leadership understanding of the Supply Planning Process") and helps resolve "unresolved issues in demand planning creating challenges in supply planning."

What & Why

The system today does not differentiate PO priority. Planning Priority POs with buffer for highest-value product ensures that strategic franchises and must-win styles get factory capacity first, with built-in buffer to absorb demand volatility without constant re-planning.

Impact: Protect revenue on top franchises, reduce churn on strategic POs

Implementation Approach

  • Define priority tiers using product segmentation (Opportunity #8)
  • Implement PO sequencing logic: priority products get capacity allocation first
  • Add configurable buffer % by priority tier to absorb demand variance
  • Build visibility into buffer utilization and coverage metrics

Prerequisites

  • Product segmentation framework (Opportunity #8)
  • Agreement on buffer methodology and funding
  • Capacity allocation governance model

Stakeholder Alignment

Alvin's #1 priority for tomorrow and definition of success. Also directly ties to Kevin's "inadequate prioritization of highest-value product" challenge.
